Basic No-Bake Cheesecake

Ingredients:

    For Crust:

    • 1 1/2 cups fine graham cracker crumbs
    • 1/2 cup sugar
    • 1/4 cup butter, melted

    For Filling:

    • 4 8oz bricks cream cheese
    • 2 cups sugar
    • 4 tsp vanilla extract
    • 4 cups Cool Whip

Instructions:

  • Prepare 10 in springform pan by cutting a circle of parchment paper to match the bottom of the pan. Spray the bottom of the pan and place the circle on the bottom. Spray the parchment paper.

    • For Crust:

    • Mix the graham cracker crumbs and the sugar in a medium bowl.
    • Add the melted butter and mix until combined.
    • Press the crumb mixture into the bottom of the pan.

    For Filling:

    • Using a stand mixer, beat cream cheese until smooth and creamy.
    • Add the sugar and vanilla extract and beat until combined.
    • Fold in the Cool Whip with a spatula until combined.

    Preparation Instructions:

    • Pour the filling into the crust and spread evenly.
    • Put paned cheesecake into fridge and allow to set at least 12 hours.
    • Carefully de-pan the cheesecake and keep refrigerated until ready to serve.

Joe's "Say Cheese" Cheesecake

CREDIT TO TRISHA YEARWOOD

Ingredients:

    For Crust:

    • 1 1/2 cups fine graham cracker crumbs
    • 1/2 cup sugar
    • 1/4 cup butter, melted

    For Filling:

    • 4 8oz bricks cream cheese
    • 2 cups sour cream
    • 4 large eggs
    • 1 1/4 cups sugar
    • 2 1/2 tbsp cornstarch
    • 2 tsp vanilla extract

    For Strawberry Sauce:

    • 1 to 1 1/4 cups halved, fresh strawberries
    • 1/4 cup sugar
    • 1 lime, zested

Instructions:

  • For the crust: Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F. Spray the bottom of a 10-inch springform pan with cooking spray; line the bottom with a round of parchment paper; and spray the paper with cooking spray. Place the pan on a sheet of heavy-duty aluminum foil and bring the foil up around the sides to enclose the seam between the bottom and the sides of the pan.*
  • Stir together the graham cracker crumbs and sugar in a mixing bowl. Add the melted butter and mix until the crumbs are coated. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of the pan and up the sides. Set aside.
  • For the filling: In a stand mixer, beat the cream cheese together with the sour cream. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Add the sugar, cornstarch, and vanilla, and beat until smooth.
  • Pour the filling into the prepared crust. Set the foil-wrapped pan inside a larger pan, and carefully pour 1/2 inch of warm water into the larger pan to create a water bath. Bake for 1 hour. Turn off the oven, open the oven door, and let the cheesecake stand in the opened oven for 1 hour. Refrigerate the cheesecake for 2 hours or overnight before removing it from the pan.*
  • For the sauce: Process the strawberries, sugar, and lime zest in a food processor or blender until smooth. Chill for at least 1 hour before serving. (The sauce is best served cold from the refrigerator.)

Tips:

  • *Put a piece of tinfoil around the bottom of the springform pan and put another piece all the way around, at least halfway up the pan, including the bottom to prevent leaks into the crust.
  • *I find that for the best results, leave the cheesecake in the pan overnight.

Cream Cheese Buttercream

Ingredients:

  • 8oz cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 cup butter, softened
  • 1 pound (approximately 4 cups) powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  • In stand mixer, beat together cream cheese, butter, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  • Add powdered sugar a little at a time until fully incorporated.
  • Beat on high until stiff peaks form or reaches desired consistency. Approximately 2-3 minutes.

Easy Chocolate Variety Truffles

CREDIT TO Raleigh Wallace

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz any kind of flavored chocolate bar.*
  • 12 oz (approximate) regular chocolate (milk, white, or dark, but with no extra flavoring)
  • 4-6 oz heavy whipping cream, depending on how soft you want your truffle to be.*

Instructions:

  • Break the 12 oz of flavored chocolate into pieces and lay it out in a pan thatâ€™s at least a few inches deep.
  • Heat the heavy whipping cream on medium/medium-high heat, stirring continuously, until it just starts to bubble. Make sure it does not burn. OR heat in the microwave, stirring every 10-15 seconds, until it is warm enough to melt the chocolate.
  • Pour the heavy whipping cream over the flavored chocolate and wait for a few minutes so the chocolate can melt. Then stir the mixture until it is smooth.
  • Put the mixture into the fridge or freezer until it is solid enough to form into balls.
  • Melt the plain chocolate. Roll the truffles into balls and dip them in the plain chocolate, then put the balls back into the fridge.*
  • Wait for the plain chocolate to solidify.

Notes:

  • *I have tried this with dark chocolate caramel Ghirardelli bars, white chocolate caramel Ghirardelli bars, and Endangered Species Forest Mint bars, and they have all turned out well.
  • *Make sure you have at least twice as much flavored chocolate as heavy whipping cream!
  • *If you have made very thick truffles (with very little whipping cream), you can skip coating them in plain chocolate, if you prefer.

No Sugar Added Apple Pie

Ingredients:

  • 3 Tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 Tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • 12 oz unsweetened apple juice concentrate
  • 6 cups thinly sliced peeled apples
  • 1 recipe for a 9-inch double crust pie

Instructions:

  • Preheat oven to 350F
  • Whisk together cornstarch, cinnamon, and 1/4 of the apple juice
  • In a saucepan over medium heat, simmer the apples in remaining apple juice until tender.
  • Add the cornstarch mixture to the apples and stir until thickened
  • Pour the mixture into the double crust pie and bake for 45 minutes

Carolina Coffee Cake

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cups shortening
  • 2 1/4 cups sifted self-rising flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup chopped nuts
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup milk

    • FOR CRUMBLE TOPPING

  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 2 tablespoons flour

Instructions:

  • Heat oven to 375 F
  • Grease and lightly flour a 10-inch cake pan
  • Cream shortening and sugar until fluffy
  • Add eggs
  • Add flour and milk alternately making 3 additions of each
  • Fold in nuts
  • Pour into pan
  • Combine all ingredients for crumble topping then put on top of cake
  • Bake for 35 minutes

Instructions:

  • Cream butter in stand mixer

White Cake

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups white sugar
  • 1 cup unsalted butter
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 Tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 3 Cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 cup milk

Instructions:

  • Preheat oven to 350F and grease and flour 2 9-inch square pans
  • Cream sugar and butter together in mixing bowl
  • Add in eggs, one at a time, beating briefly after each addition
  • Stir in vanilla
  • In a separate bowl, mix together flour and baking powder
  • Mix in wet ingredients
  • Add milk and stir until completely smooth
  • Pour batter into prepared pans
  • Bake for 30-40 minutes

Cream Cheese Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter
  • 8 ounces cream cheese
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 2 eggs at room temperature
  • 4 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 3 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  • Whisk together flour, salt, and baking powder, set aside
  • Using a stand mixer with paddle attachment, cream butter and cream cheese, add the sugar and beat until light and fluffy
  • Add egg and vanilla and mix until combined
  • Add the dry ingredients and mix on low until just combined
  • Cover bowl and set in fridge for at least one hour*
  • Heat oven to 375F
  • Portion dough into 2 tablespoon-sized balls* and place them on prepared baking sheet about 2-3 inches apart
  • Bake for 10 minutes

Notes:

  • *I find 2 hours work best
  • *They will be sticky, spray hands or scooper to make them stick less, wash when necessary

Chocolate Fudge

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups milk chocolate chips
  • 1 (14 ounce) can sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup chopped nuts (optional)

Instructions:

  • Combine all ingredients except for nuts in a large microwave-safe bowl
  • Put in microwave for 4 minutes, stirring once
  • Generously grease 8-inch square glass baking dish
  • Stir in nuts
  • Pour into prepared dish
  • Put in the fridge for at least 2 hours

Oreo Truffles

CREDIT TO RALEIGH WALLACE

Ingredients:

  • Double stuffed Oreos
  • Ghirardelli white chocolate baking bars or chips
  • Sweetened condensed milk

Instructions:

  • Separate the Oreos, keep some whole
  • Scrape icing into skillet
  • Break chocolate into chunks if not using chips and add some to the skillet
  • Add some sweetened condensed milk to the skillet, have more chocolate and icing than the sweetened condensed milk.
  • Put skillet over low heat and stir continuously until all ingredients are combined, it will be a fudge-like mixture
  • Refrigerate the fudge
  • Melt the rest of the chocolate
  • Set the Oreo cookies face down on a plate. Take some of the fudge and roll into a ball. Dip the ball into the melted chocolate, then put it on top of a cookie
  • Let cool

Crockpot Lemon-Blueberry Dessert

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans Blueberry pie filling
  • 1 box lemon pound cake mix with a glaze included
  • 5 tablespoons butter, melted

Instructions:

  • Grease a crockpot well
  • Dump both cans of the pie filling into the greased crockpot
  • Mix the melted butter and the WHOLE cake mix until a crumbly mix is formed
  • Dump the cake mix on top of the pie filling and spread in an even layer
  • Cover and turn crockpot on high for 2 1/2 hours
  • When Dessert is done, make the glaze according to box directions and drizzle over dessert

Baking Tips

  • Use room temperature ingredients for better mixing.
  • If required in recipe, preheat your oven before starting the baking process.
  • Measure ingredients accurately for the best results.
